Two-Screened Laptops

Hewlett Packard has revealed a new laptop concept, dubbed the Firefly, that has a couple new features. One, it has a 4.3-inch LCD display underneath the main display: for some reason. Two, the trackpad has been moved to the right side of the keyboard rather than located at center-stage. Interesting. Even more interesting is the question of why it ways 13 pounds.

New Ways To Connect

Metro is a concept laptop from Intel. It's a one-inch thick laptop with Core 2 Duo processor, Bluetooth support, and WiFi and WiMax radios. It is charged through a wireless charging pad. The charging pad can also charge other devices by simply placing the device on top of the notebook.

Folding Netbook Concepts

Japanese hardware and IT specialists Fujitsu recently held the Fujitsu 2011 Design Contest, and Taiwan-based designer Hao-Chun Huang took home the award with his Fujitsu Flexbook concept.

This unique design combines the ease of a tablet with the productivity of a netbook, all in a compact and convenient storage solution. The Fujitsu Flexbook lives up to its name, as it features a folding keyboard and screen. The Flexbook can fold up into a square when not in use to give the user extra portability and protection.

The Fujitsu Flexbook is also said to be waterproof but it will have to be tested once the design is complete. The combination of a tablet and netbook may just be the future of how we compute.



Transformer Laptops

Artists and graphic designers love to imagine what a next-gen Apple laptop would look like; of these concepts, the MacBook Touch by Tommaso Gecchelin is one of my favorites.

Tommaso Gecchelin’s design blends technology from notebook computers, tablets and desktops. It uses OLED flexible screens and what Gecchelin calls the iSpine, which allows the computer to bend in a number of positions without compressing the screen. An external ‘Magic Dock’ keeps the clutter of cables, ports and USBs away from the sleek MacBook Touch display.
